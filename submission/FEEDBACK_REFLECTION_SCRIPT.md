# Feedback Reflection Video Script

Target duration: 2-3 minutes. Speak naturally; the script is a guide rather than
text that must be read word for word.

## Recording Script

"The feedback on RetailPulse helped me move the project from a collection of
analyses into a more complete decision-support product.

The first important lesson was that strong modeling is not enough if a reviewer
cannot quickly understand the result. In response, I organized the dashboard
around clear business questions: overall sales performance, future demand,
customer value, churn risk, and inventory priorities. I also made model names
and validation metrics visible so the predictive outputs are easier to assess.

The second lesson was the importance of reproducibility and delivery. I connected
the analysis to a reusable Python package and pipeline, documented the setup,
included dependency and Docker configuration, and produced both dashboard and
PDF-report outputs. This makes the work easier to run, review, and extend.

The third lesson was to connect every technical result to an action. Customer
segments support targeted campaigns, churn scores prioritize retention work,
the demand forecast supports planning, and ABC plus EOQ analysis informs stock
decisions. That connection between a model and a business decision became a
central part of the final project.

If I continued the project, I would add automated data refreshes, model-drift
monitoring, user authentication, and controlled experiments to measure whether
the recommended actions improve retention and revenue.

Overall, the feedback improved both the communication and engineering quality of
RetailPulse. My biggest takeaway is that a successful analytics project must be
accurate, understandable, actionable, and easy for someone else to reproduce."

## Personalization Prompts

Before recording, replace or expand one section with the actual feedback received:

- What specific reviewer or mentor feedback changed the project?
- What was changed after that feedback?
- What tradeoff or difficulty was encountered?
- What would be done differently in the next iteration?

Do not claim feedback that was not actually received. If no formal feedback was
provided, say: "I used self-review against the project requirements and tested
the experience from a reviewer's perspective."

## Reflection Quality Check

- [ ] Names at least one real piece of feedback or an honest self-review method.
- [ ] Explains a concrete change made because of that feedback.
- [ ] Connects technical work to business value.
- [ ] Includes one limitation and one credible next step.
- [ ] Avoids unsupported claims and memorized-sounding delivery.
- [ ] Contains no private reviewer, employer, or account information.
